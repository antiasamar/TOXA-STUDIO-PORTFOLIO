EMBULLATS WINE

Packaging design for Embullats, a young Mediterranean wine brand. The visual system thoughtfully translates ideas of connection and sustainability through bold typography, a reduced color palette, and the integration of recycled fishing nets as bottle holders. A project focused on materiality, concept clarity, and contemporary brand expression.

  • CONTEXT

  • Embullats is envisioned as a young wine brand aimed at a contemporary audience, interested not only in the product but also in the values that surround it.

  • PROBLEM

  • Traditional wine identities tend to repeat historical formal codes, making it difficult to connect with new generations and leaving sustainability as a secondary or purely communicative aspect.

  • OBJECTIVE

  • To build a fresh visual system that reflects the vitality of young wine and integrates sustainability criteria structurally, aligning identity, messaging, and material application.

  • The name Embullats comes from a lively, everyday word that is very much ours. It means “entangled” or “tied up,” but it also evokes movement, bonds, and a sense of community.

    A name that speaks of connection and sustainability, reflecting both the project's origin (recycled fishing nets transformed into bottle holders) and the way we understand wine: something to be shared, that brings people together, and enjoyed without ceremony.

  • Embullats unfolds as a flexible and vibrant system, where each variety expresses its character without losing cohesion. The textile intervention and material choices integrate sustainability into the brand’s physical experience, making the packaging part of the story.
